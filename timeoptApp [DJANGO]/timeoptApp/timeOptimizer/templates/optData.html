More details to different parameters for courses
Content:
Content Aspect refers to the actual substance or material that makes up the course. This includes the topics, concepts, theories, and facts being taught. It covers everything the students need to learn and understand in a specific course or subject.
Didactic:
Didactic Aspect refers to the teaching methods, strategies, and techniques used to present the course content effectively. This includes how the material is organized, explained, and demonstrated to facilitate students' learning and understanding of the topics being taught.
Presentation:
Presentation Aspect refers to how the course material is organized, delivered, and communicated to the students. It includes elements such as the structure of the lectures, the choice of examples or case studies, the use of visual aids, and the methods of engaging students in the learning process.
Impact:
refers to the effect that a course has on students' learning and understanding of the material. It relates to the teaching methods and content delivery, and how effectively they help students grasp the concepts being taught.
